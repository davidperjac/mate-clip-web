# MateClip Product Thesis

**Working app name:** MateClip  
**Backup names:** ChessReplay, BlunderReel, MateStory, ChessCuts, PGN Studio  
**Tagline:** Turn any chess game into a viral cinematic video.  
**One-line pitch:** Paste a PGN or chess game link and MateClip creates a vertical TikTok-ready chess recap with animations, captions, chess sound effects, and optional AI voice commentary.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

MateClip is not a chess training app. It is a content-generation utility for chess players and chess creators.

The product follows the same simple transformation pattern as successful viral utility apps:

> **Easy input → magical output → immediate share/export → repeated use.**

For MateClip:

> **PGN / game link → cinematic social video → share to TikTok, Shorts, Reels, X, Discord.**

The initial product should be extremely narrow. The app should not try to become CapCut, Chess.com, Chessable, Aimchess, or an AI chess coach. The initial wedge is one repeatable job:

> “Make my game look cool enough to post.”

This is meaningfully different from existing chess apps because most chess software focuses on improvement, engines, puzzles, openings, and education. MateClip focuses on **storytelling, social expression, and content creation**.

---

## 2. The Core Insight

Most chess apps answer:

- How do I play better?
- What was the best move?
- What opening should I study?
- What puzzle should I solve?

MateClip answers:

- How do I make this game entertaining?
- How do I show my comeback, sacrifice, trap, or checkmate?
- How do I turn my chess identity into social content?

A chess player may not pay for another engine wrapper, but they may pay to turn their best games into polished videos they can show off.

The product is closer to:

- CapCut
- Captions.ai
- Opus Clip
- Remini
- Shazam-like “input → magic output” utilities

than to:

- Chess.com Game Review
- Chessable
- Lichess Study
- Aimchess
- DecodeChess

That distinction is the strategic advantage.

## 3. Target Customer

### Primary ICP: Chess content creators

These users already have repeated content needs. They may be small, medium, or aspiring creators on:

- TikTok
- YouTube Shorts
- Instagram Reels
- X
- Twitch
- Discord communities

They need short-form ideas constantly. Editing chess videos manually is annoying because it requires:

- Board capture or recreation
- Move animation
- Finding the key moments
- Adding arrows/highlights
- Adding captions
- Adding music and pacing
- Exporting in vertical format

MateClip removes that workflow.

### Secondary ICP: Online chess addicts / proud casual players

These are users who play blitz, rapid, and bullet daily. They may not call themselves creators, but they enjoy sharing:

- Brilliant moves
- Funny blunders
- Comebacks
- Queen sacrifices
- Rage quits
- Checkmates
- “900 Elo chaos” games

They may not pay for a chess course, but they may pay for expressive social output.

### Tertiary ICP: Chess coaches, clubs, and streamers

These users can use MateClip to create:

- Student-game highlights
- Club game recaps
- Tournament promo clips
- Opening trap videos
- Lesson snippets

This is not the first launch focus, but it can become a higher-value tier later.

## 4. Market Size Thesis

This market is large enough for the initial revenue goal of **$10k/month** if the product creates repeatable sharing behavior.

The math is simple:

- $7.99/month subscription → about 1,000 paying users for ~$10k MRR before store fees.
- $14.99/month subscription → about 667 paying users for ~$10k MRR before store fees.
- Hybrid subscription + credit packs can reduce dependence on a large subscriber base.

The chess audience is large. Chess.com states it has **250M+ players/members** and describes itself as the largest chess community. Its 2026 “State of Chess” update reported **250M registered users** and more than **23M human-vs-human games played on February 17, 2026**. Lichess also has a large active community and publishes open chess game data under CC0 for research/commercial use.  

The important slice is not “all chess players.” The valuable slice is:

> players who repeatedly create, post, or share chess moments.

A tiny percentage of the global chess market is enough. If MateClip can attract even a few thousand serious users through watermark-driven exports, the first $10k/month is realistic.

---

## 5. Why Existing Chess Apps Do Not Kill This Idea

### Chess.com

Chess.com is great for playing, analyzing, puzzles, lessons, bots, and community. But its product is not designed as a one-click short-form video generator. Game Review produces chess feedback, not a social-first cinematic clip.

### Lichess

Lichess is excellent for analysis, studies, games, and open-source/community chess. It is not a polished consumer content creation app.

### Chessable

Chessable is for structured training and courses. It does not solve the “make this game postable” problem.

### Aimchess / DecodeChess / other AI analysis apps

These focus on improvement and explanation. MateClip focuses on story and exportable media.

### CapCut / general video editors

CapCut can edit videos, but users still need to create the board animation and chess storytelling manually. MateClip generates the chess-specific video from PGN data automatically.

### Manual OBS / screen recording

Manual screen recording is too much friction for casual players. MateClip makes the content from the raw game record.

## 7. The Magical Moment

The first magical moment must happen inside onboarding.

Ideal flow:

1. User sees a sample chess clip autoplaying.
2. User pastes their PGN or game link.
3. User chooses a vibe: **Dramatic**, **Funny**, or **Coach Mode**.
4. App shows progress: “Finding the turning point…” → “Building the storyline…” → “Rendering your clip…”
5. User receives a polished vertical video preview.
6. User exports once for free with watermark.
7. Paywall appears after the first successful export/download/share.

The “aha” is not an analytics dashboard. It is the rendered video.

## 9. Why Retention Can Work

A one-time chess analysis app risks being “cool once.” MateClip has better retention because users can generate a new clip whenever they have a new interesting game.

Repeat loops:

- “I just played a crazy blitz game.”
- “I want to post my best win today.”
- “I need content for my chess TikTok.”
- “I want a funny recap of my friend’s blunder.”
- “I want a weekly highlight.”
- “I want to turn stream games into Shorts.”

The best customers are those who play or create often.

---

## 10. Viral Growth Loop

The viral loop should be built into the exported video.

1. User generates clip.
2. User exports with watermark if free.
3. User posts to TikTok/Reels/Shorts/X.
4. Viewers see watermark: “Made with MateClip.”
5. Viewers download app to generate their own game clip.
6. New users repeat.

The watermark must be visible but not ugly:

- Small top-right or bottom-right mark: `MateClip`.
- Animated end card: “Make yours in MateClip.”
- Free tier should keep watermark.
- Paid tier removes watermark.

## 11. Pricing Thesis

The app should use a hybrid subscription + credits model.

### Why not pure unlimited subscription?

Because each generated video has compute, storage, AI, and voice costs. Unlimited plans can be abused by heavy users.

### Why not pure credits only?

Subscriptions create recurring revenue and better MRR tracking. Credits alone can make the app feel transactional.

### Recommended model

#### Free

- 1–3 lifetime exports
- Watermark
- 720p
- Limited styles
- Optional: no AI voice or limited voice

#### Pro — $7.99/month or $49.99/year

- 25 export credits/month
- No watermark
- 1080p
- AI script captions
- Standard AI voices
- Premium themes
- Faster queue than free users

#### Creator — $19.99/month or $149.99/year

- 80 export credits/month
- Priority queue
- Longer videos
- More voice styles
- More templates
- Commercial creator license language in terms
- Bulk import later

#### Credit packs

- 10 credits: $4.99
- 30 credits: $12.99
- 100 credits: $34.99

Important App Store note: purchased credits must not expire. Monthly subscription allowances may reset by billing cycle, but purchased consumable credits should be tracked carefully and not arbitrarily expire.

---

## 12. Credit Cost Model

Suggested credit costs:

- Standard 30s watermarked video: free allowance or 1 credit
- Standard 30s no-watermark video: 1 credit
- AI voice narration: +1 credit
- 60s video: +1 credit
- 90s video: +2 credits
- Premium theme: no extra credit for subscribers, +1 for free users if enabled
- Regeneration: one free retry if the job failed technically; charge again if user changes style significantly

This keeps costs controlled and creates a fair pricing ladder.

## 13. Estimated Unit Economics

These are rough estimates and should be validated during implementation.

### Per-video estimated variable cost

| Component | Low | Typical | High | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| PGN parsing | ~$0 | ~$0 | ~$0 | Local code/server CPU only |
| Stockfish analysis | $0.001 | $0.005 | $0.03 | Depends on depth/time per move |
| AI script generation | $0.003 | $0.006 | $0.03 | Use a mini model; small prompt/output |
| OpenAI TTS voice | $0.01 | $0.02 | $0.05 | Depends on script length/model and current OpenAI pricing |
| Remotion render compute | $0.01 | $0.03 | $0.12 | Depends on worker cost, render duration, resolution |
| Remotion license if applicable | $0 | $0.01 | $0.01+ | Verify commercial requirements |
| Storage + download bandwidth | <$0.01 | <$0.01 | $0.03 | Use Cloudflare R2 for low egress risk |
| **Total** | **$0.04** | **$0.10–$0.20** | **$0.35+** | Voice and render are biggest variables |

### Margin example

If Pro is $7.99/month and includes 10 generations:

- App Store small-business net after 15% commission: ~$6.79.
- Net value per included generation: ~$0.68.
- If average variable cost per credit is $0.10–$0.18, gross margin is viable.
- If Apple commission is 30%, net is ~$6.99, still viable if average cost stays below ~$0.20 per credit.

Protect the business by:

- Limiting included credits
- Charging extra for voice/longer clips
- Compressing videos efficiently
- Keeping MVP audio to owned SFX and optional paid AI voice
- Not using AI video generation tools like Runway/Pika for core output

## 14. MVP Scope Thesis

The MVP must prove only one thing:

> Users will paste a chess game and share/export the generated vertical video.

### MVP must include

- PGN paste input
- One sample onboarding video
- One user-generated video render
- One vertical 9:16 template
- Basic move animation
- Captions
- Key moment detection
- Sound effects
- One music track
- Watermark
- Free export
- Simple paywall
- Basic credit tracking

### MVP should not include

- Full mobile video editor
- Timeline editing
- Drag/drop captions
- Custom voice cloning
- Custom music upload
- Deep engine analysis
- OAuth with Chess.com or Lichess
- Multi-game import
- Creator dashboard
- Team collaboration
- Web app

## 18. Risks

### Risk 1: Output quality is not good enough

This is the biggest risk. If the generated clips look cheap, users will not share them.

Mitigation:

- Focus on one excellent template, not many mediocre ones.
- Use high-quality motion, sound design, and caption styling.
- Create 20 sample clips before launching.

### Risk 2: Video rendering is slow or unreliable

Mitigation:

- Render 720p for free users and 1080p for paid.
- Keep MVP videos under 45 seconds.
- Use queue status and retry logic.
- Store generated files for re-download.

### Risk 3: Costs rise with heavy users

Mitigation:

- Use credits.
- Charge extra for voice and long videos.
- Compress output.
- Make generated music optional, not default.

### Risk 4: App becomes too complex

Mitigation:

- No timeline editor.
- No CapCut clone.
- Only template choices.
- “Automatic first, customization second.”

### Risk 5: Chess site import is unreliable

Mitigation:

- MVP starts with PGN paste.
- Chess.com/Lichess imports are convenience features later.
- Provide simple instructions: “Copy PGN from Chess.com/Lichess and paste here.”

---

## 19. Product Principles

1. **Automatic beats editable.** Users came for magic, not work.
2. **Shareability beats accuracy.** Engine accuracy matters, but dramatic clarity matters more.
3. **One excellent template beats ten average templates.**
4. **Watermark exports are growth.** Do not block the first free share.
5. **No chess platform creep.** Avoid becoming another training app.
6. **The final video is the product.** Everything else supports that.
7. **Charge for removal of friction.** No watermark, HD, voice, more credits, and priority queue.

---

## 20. Strategic Recommendation

Build MateClip as a two-week MVP with PGN paste only and one cinematic template. Do not start with AI voice, full editing, OAuth imports, or multiple themes. Prove the viral loop first.

After MVP validation, add in this order:

1. AI script/captions improvement
2. OpenAI TTS voice narration
3. More templates
4. Chess.com username/latest-games import
5. Lichess link import
6. Creator tier and bulk tools
7. Weekly “best clip from my games” automation

The first real milestone is not revenue. It is this:

> 100 users generate clips, 30+ export/share, and at least 5 ask for more credits/templates/voice.

If that happens, the app is worth continuing.
